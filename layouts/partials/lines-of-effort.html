Our Lines of Effort

Resonant Knowledge Lab pursues a focused set of efforts that connect research, governance, and applied systems development. We are actively advancing our core programs while envisioning additional areas that will grow as partnerships and capacity expand.

Active Programs

Open Protocols

Apply and refine public-interest protocols like the Model Context Protocol (MCP) for secure AI–knowledge interaction. We help define how reasoning systems can interact securely and verifiably with governed knowledge domains—ensuring privacy, provenance, and organizational control.

Reference Toolkits

Build open-source adapters, audit logging, and reproducible on-premises stacks organizations can deploy. Our reference implementations demonstrate practical examples of secure AI integration that others can adapt, deploy, and audit.

Field Pilots

Partner with organizations to deploy real-world systems that bridge research and institutional knowledge. Through applied pilots—from marine science to municipal planning—we validate practical, secure AI integration.

Emerging Areas

Governance Frameworks

Publish consent, data licensing, and access-control templates. We're developing governance tools that help organizations define who can access what knowledge, under which conditions, and with what oversight.

Applied Research

Study how human–machine reasoning can responsibly contribute to knowledge creation. Through partnerships, we examine how AI-supported reasoning enhances learning and decision-making.

Education & Outreach

Build capacity through workshops and training labs on ethical AI and knowledge governance. From technical training to red/blue-team exercises, we help organizations implement responsible AI practices.
